<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The 3D Integrated Circuit market comprises three types of technologies - Through Silicon Via (TSV), Silicon Interposer, and Through Glass Via (TGV). TSV involves vertically stacking multiple silicon wafers and connecting them through tiny via holes, enabling high-performance and compact designs. Silicon Interposer is an alternative method that uses a silicon wafer as an interconnect layer for integrating chips. On the other hand, TGV replaces the silicon interposer with a glass wafer, offering superior electrical performance and thermal management capabilities. These technologies are revolutionizing the semiconductor industry by enabling higher efficiency and functionality in smaller form factors.</p></p>
