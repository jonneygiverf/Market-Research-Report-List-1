<p><p>The glass spacers market includes various types of spacers used in insulated glass units (IGUs) for enhancing energy efficiency. Warm edge spacers minimize heat transfer at the edges, reducing condensation and enhancing insulation. Cold edge spacers are traditional aluminum or steel spacers with lower efficiency. TPS spacers combine rigid and flexible materials for improved performance. Flexible spacers are made of flexible foam or rubber, providing better thermal insulation. Stiff spacers, such as stainless steel or plastic, offer rigidity and durability. All these spacers play a crucial role in maintaining energy efficiency in IGUs.</p></p>
