<p><p>A split eddy current displacement sensor is a type of non-contact measurement device used to detect and measure small distances or displacements between two conductive objects. It functions based on the principle of eddy current induction, where a magnetic field is created by an alternating current passed through a coil. Any changes between the coil and the target object's electrical conductivity or distance will result in changes to the eddy currents and subsequently the impedance of the coil.</p><p>The split eddy current displacement sensor market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for accurate and precise measurement devices in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and electronics is driving market growth.
