<p><p>A spinal cage, also known as an intervertebral cage or a fusion cage, is a medical device used in spinal fusion surgeries to stabilize the spine and promote bone growth. It is designed to be implanted between the vertebrae to maintain normal spinal alignment and facilitate bone fusion. Spinal cages are typically made of biocompatible materials such as titanium or polyetheretherketone (PEEK).</p><p>The global spinal cage market is expected to experience significant growth over the forecast period. Factors driving the market growth include the rising prevalence of spinal disorders and increasing demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures. Additionally, advancements in implant technologies, such as 3D printing and customizable cages, are fueling market expansion.</p><p>Market analysis suggests that the spinal cage market is witnessing a shift from traditional open surgeries to minimally invasive procedures. Minimally invasive spinal surgeries offer benefits such as smaller incisions, reduced blood loss, shorter hospital stays, and quicker recovery time. This trend towards minimally invasive procedures is expected to increase the adoption of spinal cages.</p><p>Moreover, the growing aging population globally contributes to the increasing incidence of age-related spine disorders such as degenerative disc disease and spondylolisthesis. <p><p>The spinal cage market comprises types such as Polyetheretherketone (PEEK) and Titanium. PEEK cages are made from a high-performance thermoplastic polymer known for its biocompatibility and mechanical strength. These cages provide stability and support to the spine during fusion procedures. On the other hand, Titanium cages are made from a lightweight and durable metal that offers excellent biological compatibility and fusion properties. Both PEEK and Titanium cages aim to improve patient outcomes by promoting bone growth and maintaining spinal alignment, but differ in material composition and specific characteristics.</p></p>
