<p><p>Architectural concrete, also known as decorative concrete or stamped concrete, refers to the use of concrete in architectural applications beyond its conventional use as a basic building material. Architectural concrete can be found in a wide range of applications, including residential, commercial, and public spaces, such as driveways, patios, walkways, walls, and floors.</p><p>The future outlook of the architectural concrete market looks promising. <p><p>Architectural concrete market encompasses various types of concrete finishes used in the construction industry. Stamped concrete refers to the process of imprinting patterns or textures onto freshly poured concrete. Stained concrete involves using chemical or acid-based stains to add color and create unique designs. Concrete overlays are thin layers applied on existing concrete surfaces to enhance appearance and durability. Colored concrete involves adding pigments to the mixture to achieve desired colors. Polished concrete involves grinding and polishing the surface for a glossy finish. Epoxy coating is applied as a protective layer to enhance resistance to chemicals and abrasion. Other types may include exposed aggregate, textured concrete, or terrazzo finishes.</p></p>
