<p><p>The passenger vehicle aluminum alloy wheel market has three types: casting, forging, and other markets. Casting refers to the process of pouring molten aluminum into a mold to form the wheel shape. This method is widely used due to its low cost and high flexibility in design. Forging involves shaping the wheel by applying pressure and heat to a solid aluminum billet. This method produces wheels with enhanced strength and durability. The category of "other markets" includes alternative processes like flow-forming and hybrid techniques, which offer unique advantages and characteristics.</p></p>
