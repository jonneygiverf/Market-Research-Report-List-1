<p><p>Wired bioimpedance devices are categorized into two types: single-frequency and multiple-frequency bioimpedance devices. Single-frequency devices measure the impedance of biological tissues using a fixed frequency. They are simpler and less expensive but may provide limited information. Multiple-frequency devices, on the other hand, utilize various frequencies to analyze tissue impedance, offering more comprehensive and accurate results. These devices are more advanced and expensive. Both types are wired, meaning they require physical connections to transmit data, ensuring reliable and real-time measurements for medical applications.</p></p>
