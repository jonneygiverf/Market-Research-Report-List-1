<p><p>Railway signaling systems refer to the technologies and equipment used to ensure safe and efficient train operations. In the market, there are three major types: Communication-Based Train Control (CBTC), Positive Train Control (PTC), and Automatic Train Control (ATC). CBTC utilizes advanced communication systems to control and monitor train movements with higher accuracy and capacity. PTC combines communication and location technologies to prevent train collisions and derailments. ATC employs various sensors and control mechanisms to manage train speed and improve safety. These systems play a crucial role in enhancing railway transportation by optimizing track capacity and ensuring passenger security.</p></p>

Outside the station, the signaling system plays a crucial role in controlling the movement of trains on tracks, preventing collisions, regulating train speed, and ensuring proper signaling at crossings. Overall, the signaling system improves railway safety, reliability, and operational efficiency both inside and outside the station.</p></p>
