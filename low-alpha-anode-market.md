<p><p>The term "Low Alpha Anode" refers to a type of anode material used in the semiconductor industry, specifically in the production of high-performance integrated circuits (ICs). Anodes are an essential component in the IC manufacturing process as they are responsible for controlling the flow of electrical current.</p><p>Low Alpha Anodes are designed to have low levels of alpha emission, which refers to the emission of alpha particles (helium nuclei) during the decay of radioactive isotopes. This is crucial because alpha particles can cause disruptions and defects in semiconductor devices, leading to performance degradation or even failure.</p><p>The Low Alpha Anode Market is experiencing steady growth, expected to reach a CAGR of 11.5% during the forecast period.
