<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive (DCT) Dual Clutch Transmission market is divided into two types: wet DCT and dry DCT. Wet DCTs use oil to cool and lubricate the clutch system, resulting in better performance, smoother shifting, and increased durability. Dry DCTs, on the other hand, do not require oil and rely on friction materials for cooling and lubrication. While dry DCTs are generally cheaper and more fuel-efficient, wet DCTs offer improved overall functionality and are commonly used in high-performance vehicles.</p></p>
