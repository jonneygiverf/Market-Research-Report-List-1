<p><p>Magnesium alloys are categorized into two main types: cast alloys and wrought alloys. Cast alloys are formed through molten magnesium being poured into a mold and solidified. These alloys possess good fluidity and are commonly used for components that require complex shapes. On the other hand, wrought alloys are created through mechanical deformation, such as rolling or forging, which enhances their strength and mechanical properties. Wrought alloys are utilized for applications that demand higher strength, durability, and resistance to impact or vibration.</p></p>
