<p><p>The solvent-based pharmaceutical ink market comprises three main types: solids, semi-solids, and liquids. Solids refer to powdered inks that are typically mixed with a solvent to form a paste or liquid ink before application. Semi-solids are thicker in consistency and are commonly used for imprinting on tablets or capsules. Liquids are already in a liquid form and are directly applied using various printing techniques. These different types cater to specific pharmaceutical packaging requirements, ensuring legibility, adherence, and durability during the product lifecycle.</p></p>
