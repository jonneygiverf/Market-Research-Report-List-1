<p><p>Hot Strip Mill:</p><p>A hot strip mill is a rolling mill that is used to convert slabs into hot rolled coils. Slabs, which are a semi-finished product, are heated and then passed through a series of rollers to obtain the desired thickness and shape. This process not only reduces the thickness of the slabs, but also significantly improves the mechanical properties of the material, making it suitable for a variety of applications such as automotive, construction, and packaging.</p><p>Cold Rolling Mill:</p><p>A cold rolling mill is used to roll down hot rolled coils further to obtain the desired thickness and surface quality. Unlike hot strip mills, cold rolling mills operate at room temperature, which results in better control over the process and final product properties. Cold rolled coils find applications in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and consumer electronics, where tight tolerances and excellent surface finish are required.</p><p>The future outlook of the hot strip mill and cold rolling mill market is expected to be promising.
