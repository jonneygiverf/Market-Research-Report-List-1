<p><p>Medical enteral feeding tubes are devices that are inserted into the stomach or small intestine to provide nutrition and medication to patients who are unable to consume food orally. The market is driven by the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer and neurological disorders, which often lead to difficulty in swallowing and require enteral feeding. For instance, the development of low-profile, more flexible, and easier to place feeding tubes have made enteral feeding more convenient for both healthcare providers and patients.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has increased the demand for enteral feeding tubes, as critically ill patients require nutritional support during their recovery.
