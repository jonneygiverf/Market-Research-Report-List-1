<p><p>HVAC refrigerant is a substance or mixture used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems that undergoes a phase transition from a gas to a liquid and vice versa, while absorbing and releasing heat. It plays a crucial role in the heat transfer process and is responsible for maintaining the desired temperature of the surroundings.</p><p>The HVAC refrigerant market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years due to the increasing demand for energy-efficient and environmentally-friendly cooling solutions. The market is driven by various factors such as the rising need for HVAC systems in residential, commercial, and industrial sectors, stringent government regulations regarding energy efficiency and environmental concerns, and the continuous advancements in HVAC technology.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One notable trend is the growing adoption of natural refrigerants, including hydrofluoro-olefins (HFOs), hydrocarbons (HCs), and ammonia, owing to their low global warming potential (GWP) and ozone depletion potential (ODP). These environmentally-friendly refrigerants are being preferred over the traditional hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs) and hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs).</p><p>Another trend driving the market is the increasing focus on energy efficiency. HVAC systems account for a significant portion of energy consumption in buildings, and therefore, there is a growing demand for refrigerants that offer improved energy efficiency and reduce the overall energy costs.</p><p>Overall, the HVAC refrigerant market is anticipated to witness a steady growth rate during the forecast period. According to the provided information, the market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.3%, driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient and environmentally-friendly cooling solutions, the adoption of natural refrigerants, and the focus on energy efficiency.</p></p>

Hydrocarbons are gaining popularity as they are environmentally friendly alternatives, with lower global warming potential.
