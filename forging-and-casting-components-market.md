<p><p>Forging and casting are two different manufacturing processes used to produce components for various industries. </p><p>Forging involves shaping metal by applying compressive forces to it, typically with the use of hammers or presses. This process creates components with superior strength and structural integrity compared to other manufacturing methods. Forged components are commonly used in applications where durability and reliability are key, such as in automotive, aerospace, and oil and gas industries.</p><p>On the other hand, casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold and allowing it to solidify. This process is suitable for producing complex shapes and large components. Cast components may not have the same strength as forged ones, but they often offer other benefits such as cost-effectiveness and design flexibility. Casting is widely utilized in industries such as construction, manufacturing, and energy.</p><p>The market for forging and casting components is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years.
