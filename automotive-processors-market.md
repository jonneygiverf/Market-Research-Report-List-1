<p><p>The automotive processors market includes two main types: 8-bits and 16-bits processors. </p><p>8-bit processors have a smaller word length and can handle data and instructions in smaller chunks, making them suitable for simple and low-cost automotive applications. They are used in tasks such as controlling basic functions like dashboard displays, keyless entry, and basic engine management.</p><p>On the other hand, 16-bit processors have a larger word length and are capable of handling more complex operations. They are used in advanced automotive systems like fuel injection, anti-lock brakes, and transmission control to process larger amounts of data quickly and efficiently.</p></p>
