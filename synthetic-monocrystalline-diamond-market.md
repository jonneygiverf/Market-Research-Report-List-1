<p><p>The Synthetic Monocrystalline Diamond (SMD) market includes two main types of manufacturing processes: High Pressure High Temperature (HPHT) and Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD). HPHT involves subjecting carbon under high pressure and temperature to create diamond crystals. This process is commonly used for industrial applications due to its ability to produce large and high-quality diamonds. On the other hand, CVD involves the deposition of carbon atoms onto a substrate through a gas-phase process. This method is often preferred for creating smaller diamond crystals used in electronics and semiconductor industries.</p></p>
