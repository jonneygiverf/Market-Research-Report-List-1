<p><p>Polyvinyl butyral (PVB) resins are a type of synthetic thermoplastic polymer that is primarily used as an intermediate layer in laminated glass manufacturing. These resins provide excellent adhesion, toughness, and transparency which make them suitable for various applications in automotive, construction, and architectural industries. PVB resins are commonly used as an interlayer in windshields, safety glass, and bullet-resistant glass due to their high impact resistance and ability to hold glass fragments together in case of breakage.</p><p>The polyvinyl butyral resins market is projected to witness steady growth in the coming years. The increasing consumption of laminated glass in automotive and construction industries is expected to drive the demand for PVB resins. The automotive sector, in particular, is experiencing a surge in demand for laminated glass due to the growing focus on safety features and regulations mandating the use of laminated windshields. Additionally, the rising construction activities and infrastructure development projects around the world are further fueling the demand for PVB resins in the architectural sector.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing adoption of PVB resins in solar panel encapsulation is likely to open up new growth opportunities for the market.
