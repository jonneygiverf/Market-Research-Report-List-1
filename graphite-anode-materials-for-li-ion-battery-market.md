<p><p>Graphite anode materials are widely used in Li-ion batteries. They can be categorized into two types: artificial graphite and natural graphite. Artificial graphite is produced through a high-temperature process using carbon precursors, while natural graphite is sourced directly from mines. Both have distinct characteristics that make them suitable for battery applications. Artificial graphite offers better consistency, higher capacity, and longer cycle life, while natural graphite provides lower cost and higher energy density. The market for graphite anode materials is driven by the growing demand for Li-ion batteries in various industries, such as electric vehicles and portable electronics.</p></p>
