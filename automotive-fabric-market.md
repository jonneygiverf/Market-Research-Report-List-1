<p><p>The automotive fabric market encompasses woven, nonwoven, and composites materials. Woven fabrics are made by interlacing vertical and horizontal yarns, offering excellent tensile strength but limited flexibility. Nonwoven fabrics are produced by bonding or felting fibers together, providing low cost, light weight, and high durability. Composites are materials composed of two or more components, such as fabrics combined with resins or polymers, offering enhanced strength, stiffness, and resistance to heat and chemicals. All these fabric types are widely used in automotive interiors, seat covers, headliners, door panels, and upholstery.</p></p>
