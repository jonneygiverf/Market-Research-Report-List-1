<p><p>Automotive cable materials refer to the materials used in the manufacturing of cables used in vehicles. The key factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for electric vehicles (EVs) and the rising automotive production globally. With the transition towards EVs, the demand for automotive cable materials is expected to soar due to the heavy use of cables in electric powertrains and charging infrastructure. These materials offer better insulation and conductivity properties, enabling efficient transmission of electrical signals in vehicles. These cables release fewer toxic gases during combustion, reducing the risk of fire hazards.</p><p>Overall, with the expected CAGR of 13.2% during the forecast period, the automotive cable materials market is anticipated to witness significant growth, driven by the increasing adoption of EVs, lightweight materials, and technological advancements in the automotive industry.</p></p>

<p><p>The automotive cable materials market consists of various types of materials used in manufacturing automotive cables. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is commonly used due to its flexibility and insulation properties. XLPE (cross-linked polyethylene) is known for its high-temperature resistance and excellent mechanical strength. TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) offers flexibility and resistance to oil and chemicals. PPE (polyphenylene ether) is used for its excellent flame resistance and electrical properties. Other materials used include EPDM (ethylene propylene diene monomer) and HFFR (halogen-free flame retardant), which offer specific characteristics such as weather resistance and fire retardancy, respectively.</p></p>
